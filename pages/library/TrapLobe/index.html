Grasping Structure - Trap Lobe

Inspiration

The Venus flytrap’s method of trapping prey is fascinating. By stretching its leaves and storing elastic energy, it can quickly close its trap when triggered. The stretching of the leaves builds up potential mechanical energy, which is released in a hydraulic movement when the trap snaps shut. This mechanism doesn’t rely on muscles but rather on the plant’s ability to store and release energy in an efficient, rapid manner.

Reference：
Stretched Leaves Power Rapid Closure

How it Works

As the temperature increases, the Trap Lobe structure snaps open, releasing stored energy.

Sample Conditions:

  • Temperature change from 35°C to 25°C
  • Humidity remained constant (~30%)

Sample Parameters:

  • Strips thickness: 0.2 mm；
